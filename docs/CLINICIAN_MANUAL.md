## What This System Does

This clinical knowledge base provides **evidence-based decision support** for reproductive health professionals in resource-limited settings. It includes:

- **Clinical Guidelines** - WHO protocols for obstetrics, contraception, STI management
- **Drug Reference** - Dosing, contraindications, and pregnancy safety for essential medicines
- **Emergency Protocols** - Step-by-step guidance for complications like postpartum hemorrhage
- **Triage Tools** - Risk assessment for maternal and neonatal emergencies

**IMPORTANT:** This is a DECISION SUPPORT tool, not a substitute for clinical judgment. You remain responsible for patient care decisions.

### Logging In

## How to Search for Information

### Method 1: Clinical Query Search

Best for: Finding management protocols or decision guidance

**Steps:**
1. Tap the **Search** button or **Clinical Chat** icon
2. Type your question in natural language:
   - "How do I manage postpartum hemorrhage?"
   - "What is the dose of oxytocin for AMTSL?"
   - "Management of eclampsia in labor"
3. Tap **Search** or press Enter
4. Results appear with relevant guidance and page citations

**Tips:**
- Use key clinical terms: hemorrhage, eclampsia, sepsis, etc.
- Include patient context: "preeclampsia at 30 weeks" or "GBS-positive mother"
- Ask about specific drugs, doses, or procedures

### Method 2: Drug Lookup

Best for: Finding specific medication information

**Steps:**
1. Tap the **Drug Lookup** button
2. Enter the drug name: oxytocin, misoprostol, magnesium sulfate, etc.
3. Results show:
   - Standard dose and route
   - Indication and duration
   - Contraindications (who should NOT receive this drug)
   - Warnings (side effects, pregnancy safety, breastfeeding)
   - WHO Essential Medicines List status

**Pregnancy Safety Indicator:**
- 🟢 **Green:** Safe throughout pregnancy
- 🟡 **Yellow:** Use with caution; check specific trimester
- 🔴 **Red:** Contraindicated (do not use in pregnancy)

### Method 3: Emergency Protocols

Best for: Managing acute complications during labor

**Steps:**
1. Tap **Emergency Protocols**
2. Select your clinical scenario:
   - Postpartum Hemorrhage
   - Eclampsia/Preeclampsia
   - Neonatal Resuscitation
   - Sepsis/Infection
3. Follow step-by-step algorithm with triage scoring

**Red buttons indicate EMERGENCY actions** - these require immediate intervention.

### Method 4: Decision Trees

Best for: Determining best contraceptive method or management approach

**Steps:**
1. Tap **Decision Support**
2. Answer clinical questions about the patient
3. The system guides you through criteria and recommends method
4. See Why columns for rationale behind recommendations

## Pregnancy Safety & Breastfeeding

### Drug Categories

When looking up medications, you'll see pregnancy categories:

| Category | Meaning | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **A** | Safe throughout pregnancy | Magnesium sulfate |
| **B** | Animal studies OK, human data limited | Amoxicillin, Dexamethasone |
| **C** | Animal or human data show some risk; use if benefit > risk | Nifedipine, Labetalol |
| **D** | Evidence of fetal risk, but may use in emergencies | Magnesium sulfate in eclampsia |
| **X** | Contraindicated - do NOT use in pregnancy | Misoprostol (except medical abortion) |

### Breastfeeding Safety

Look for the **Lactation Risk** indicator:

- 🟢 **Safe (L1-L2):** No impact on infant, safe to breastfeed
- 🟡 **Caution (L3):** Limited data, monitor infant
- 🔴 **Not safe (L4-L5):** Do NOT breastfeed while using

## Triage & Risk Assessment Tools

### Maternal Danger Signs Scoring

**When to use:** Any pregnant or recently-delivered woman with concerning symptoms

**How to use:**
1. Tap **Triage** or **Danger Signs Assessment**
2. Enter vital signs:
   - Blood pressure, heart rate, temperature
   - Respiratory rate, oxygen saturation
3. Check symptoms:
   - Vaginal bleeding (if present, how much?)
   - Abdominal pain, headache, visual changes
   - Breathing difficulty, chest pain
4. Tap **Calculate Score**

**Results show:**
- 🟢 **GREEN:** No danger signs, routine care
- 🟡 **YELLOW:** Caution, monitor closely
- 🟠 **ORANGE:** Urgent, escalate care
- 🔴 **RED:** EMERGENCY, immediate intervention needed

### Apgar Scoring

**When to use:** For every newborn at 1 and 5 minutes

**How to use:**
1. At 1 minute of age, assess infant on 5 criteria
2. Tap **Apgar Calculator**
3. For each category, select the score (0, 1, or 2):
   - **Appearance** (skin color)
   - **Pulse** (heart rate)
   - **Grimace** (reflex response)
   - **Activity** (muscle tone)
   - **Respiration** (breathing effort)
4. Tap **Calculate**

**Interpretation:**
- **9-10 (Excellent):** Vigorous, routine care
- **7-8 (Good):** Normal, monitor closely
- **4-6 (Fair):** Requires stimulation, oxygen, intervention
- **0-3 (Poor):** EMERGENCY resuscitation needed

## Drug Interactions

### Checking for Drug Interactions

**When to use:** Before prescribing multiple drugs

**How to use:**
1. Tap **Drug Interactions**
2. Enter the drugs you want to check:
   - First drug: Misoprostol
   - Second drug: Oxytocin
3. Tap **Check Interaction**

**Results show severity:**
- 🟢 **No interaction** - Safe to combine
- 🟡 **Minor** - Can use but monitor
- 🟠 **Significant** - Use alternative if possible
- 🔴 **Contraindicated** - Do NOT combine

**Example result:**
```
Misoprostol + Oxytocin = CONTRAINDICATED
Reason: Both are uterotonics; causes tetanic uterus, uterine rupture risk
Recommendation: Use one agent only. If misoprostol already given, avoid oxytocin.
```

## Accessing Emergency Protocols

### Step-by-Step Algorithms

For common emergencies:

**Postpartum Hemorrhage Management:**
1. **Assessment**
   - Measure blood loss
   - Check vital signs, urine output
   - Assess for shock signs

2. **Initial Actions**
   - Tap uterine fundus
   - Empty bladder
   - Empty uterus of clots
   - Initiate IV fluids

3. **Pharmacological Management**
   - Oxytocin 10 IU IV/IM
   - If fails: Ergot 0.5 mg IM
   - If fails: Misoprostol 600-800 μg PR

4. **Surgical Management**
   - If medical measures fail
   - Prepare for transfer to OR
   - Do NOT delay transport

Each step shows **who performs it**, **what supplies needed**, **when to escalate**.

## Troubleshooting

### "Bundle Download Failed"

**Problem:** Initial bundle download not completing

**Solutions:**
1. Check internet connection (WiFi recommended for large files)
2. Try again in a location with stronger signal
3. Free up device storage (need 100+ MB free)
4. Contact IT support if problem persists

### "Search Returning No Results"

**Problem:** Your query doesn't match any documents

**Solutions:**
1. Try simpler terms: "PPH" instead of "postpartum hemorrhage hemorrhage"
2. Search for common conditions: "sepsis", "hypertension", "contraception"
3. Try drug search instead: Look up specific medication
4. Contact clinical coordinator if content is missing

### "Drug Not in System"

**Problem:** Drug you need is not in the formulary

**Solutions:**
1. Check if it's under alternative name (generic vs brand)
2. Use **Drug Interactions** with similar drug in same class
3. **Contact your clinical director** to request drug be added
4. Refer to your facility's local formulary

### App Crashes or Freezes

**Problem:** App not responding

**Solutions:**
1. Force close app and restart
2. Ensure app is updated to latest version
3. Restart your device
4. If persistent, delete and reinstall app (bundle will re-download)

## Best Practices

### Before Patient Care

1. **Search relevant protocol** - Familiarize yourself with current guidance
2. **Check patient-specific factors** - Pregnancy status, allergies, comorbidities
3. **Gather supplies** - Verify you have drugs, equipment before starting
4. **Brief your team** - Discuss plan with midwife, nurse, porter, etc.

### During Patient Care

1. **Document findings** - Record vital signs, assessments, interventions
2. **Follow protocol** - Use step-by-step algorithms, don't skip steps
3. **Communicate** - Alert team if escalation needed
4. **Use evidence-based practices** - Prefer WHO protocols over local custom

### After Patient Care

1. **Complete documentation** - Full notes in patient record
2. **Debrief team** - Discuss case, what went well, what could improve
3. **Report incidents** - If adverse event or system error, report to supervisor
4. **Follow up patient** - Ensure post-operative or post-partum care
